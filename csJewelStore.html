Multi‑Brand E‑commerce Platform

Building a scalable Shopify‑based multi‑brand retail platform for a US jewelry client with custom integrations, BOPIS, repair services, and SEO optimization – all within a $25,000 budget.

Client US Jewelry Store (Multi‑Brand)
Budget $25,000
Duration 4 Months
Team 5 Members
Role Project Manager / Lead Developer
Industry Retail · Jewelry

Executive Summary

The client, a well‑established jewelry retailer in the US with multiple in‑house brands, needed a unified digital presence to manage online sales, in‑store purchases, and buy‑online‑pick‑up‑in‑store (BOPIS). They also required seamless handling of gold/diamond procurement, repair services, and custom jewelry creation. We delivered a custom Shopify‑based platform with integrated POS, appointment scheduling, and loyalty modules, resulting in 3× revenue growth, 150% traffic increase, and a 4.8★ user rating.

Client Background

A family‑owned jewelry store chain with 3 physical locations in the US, carrying 10+ exclusive brands. They were selling gold, diamonds, and custom‑made pieces, but their online presence was limited to a static catalog. They wanted to modernize their operations, attract younger buyers, and offer omnichannel convenience.

Business Problem

  • No unified e‑commerce platform – sales were split between stores and a basic website
  • Manual inventory management leading to stock discrepancies
  • Lack of integration between online orders and in‑store pickup/repairs
  • No loyalty or engagement features to retain customers
  • SEO deficiencies – low visibility for high‑intent jewelry searches
  • Limited budget ($25,000) required a cost‑effective, scalable solution

Solution Overview

Shopify Plus

Multi‑brand storefront with custom Liquid themes, optimized for conversions and mobile‑first experience.

BOPIS & Inventory Sync

Real‑time stock updates across all locations, enabling seamless buy‑online‑pickup‑in‑store and in‑store returns.

Custom Services

Integrated modules for jewelry repair, watch service, custom design requests, and gold/diamond procurement.

Architecture & Technology

High‑level Architecture

Shopify frontend + custom Node.js middleware + third‑party POS & ERP connectors

Shopify Liquid Node.js REST APIs Webhooks SEO Google Analytics 4 Hotjar

Project Delivery

Discovery

2‑week discovery with stakeholder workshops, user journey mapping, and technical audit

Design & Build

8‑week agile sprints covering theme development, integrations, and custom apps

Testing & UAT

2‑week QA, load testing, and user acceptance testing with store staff

Go‑Live & Hypercare

Phased rollout with 4‑week hypercare support and training

Challenges & Lessons Learned

  • Integration complexity: Synchronizing Shopify with legacy POS required custom middleware; we used webhooks and a lightweight Node.js service.
  • Budget constraints: We prioritized core features (BOPIS, repair modules) and used Shopify Apps for loyalty instead of building from scratch.
  • User adoption: Store staff needed training on the new system – multiple training sessions for Shopify Adming and making changes in the website.
  • SEO: We implemented structured data, optimized meta tags, and created a blog for jewelry care tips, which boosted organic traffic significantly.

Future Roadmap

  • AI‑powered product recommendations based on purchase history
  • Augmented reality (AR) try‑on for rings and necklaces
  • Subscription‑based jewelry care plans
  • Integration with CRM for personalized email campaigns
  • Expansion to marketplaces (Amazon, Etsy) via Shopify connectors
