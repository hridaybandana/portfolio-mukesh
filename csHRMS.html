HRMS & Employee Portal

Comprehensive HR management system with attendance, leave, performance, and payroll modules – built internally on Microsoft Power Platform for 500+ users with a $1,000 budget.

Client Internal (Our Organization)
Budget $1,000
Timeline 6 Months
Team 3 Members
Role Project Manager / Lead Developer
Platform Microsoft Power Platform

Executive Summary

Our organization, a mid‑sized enterprise with 500+ employees, was struggling with fragmented HR processes – attendance was tracked via spreadsheets, leave requests were managed via email, and performance reviews were conducted manually. With a minimal budget of $1,000 and a 6‑month timeline, we decided to build an internal HR Management System (HRMS) using the Microsoft Power Platform (Power Apps, Power Automate, Power BI, and Dataverse). The solution consolidated attendance, leave, performance, and payroll into a single employee portal, reducing HR processing time by 80% and ensuring 100% compliance with company policies.

Internal Background

Our company, a fast‑growing tech firm with offices in three countries, had outgrown its manual HR processes. The HR team of 4 people spent over 30 hours per week on data entry, leave tracking, and payroll calculations. Employees frequently complained about the lack of transparency and delays in approvals. We recognised that a low‑cost, low‑code solution could transform our internal operations without significant capital expenditure.

Business Problem

  • Attendance records were maintained in Excel and having multiple issues, leading to errors and disputes
  • Leave requests were submitted via email, with no central tracking or visibility
  • Performance appraisals were paper‑based, making it difficult to track progress
  • Payroll calculations were prone to mistakes and consumed valuable HR time
  • No employee self‑service portal, causing frequent HR inquiries
  • Budget constraints ($1,000) ruled out off‑the‑shelf enterprise solutions

Solution Overview

Attendance & Time Tracking

Power Apps canvas app with check‑in/out via mobile or web, integrated with Dataverse for real‑time tracking and reporting.

Leave Management

Automated request‑approval workflow with balance tracking, leave calendars, and email notifications via Power Automate.

Performance & Payroll

Performance review forms with manager feedback, integrated with payroll calculation and reporting dashboards in Power BI.

Architecture & Technology

Power Platform Architecture

Power Apps (Canvas & Model‑driven) + Power Automate flows + Dataverse + Power BI dashboards

Power Apps Power Automate Power BI Dataverse SharePoint Microsoft 365

Project Delivery – Agile & Cost‑Conscious

Sprint Planning

8 two‑week sprints, prioritising core modules (attendance → leave → performance → payroll).

Low‑Code Development

Leveraged Power Apps to build rapidly, reusing components and minimising custom code.

Cost Optimisation

Used existing Microsoft 365 licences; no additional infrastructure costs, staying within $1,000 budget.

User‑Centric Design

Conducted internal surveys and prototypes to ensure the portal met employee needs.

Despite the tight budget, we delivered all core features on time. The project was managed using Azure DevOps, with daily stand‑ups and bi‑weekly demos to internal stakeholders.

Deployment & Go‑Live

UAT

2 weeks of internal testing with a pilot group of 50 employees; feedback incorporated.

Training

Created video tutorials and quick‑reference guides; conducted town hall sessions.

Phased Rollout

Launched in phases: attendance first, then leave, performance, and finally payroll.

Hypercare

4 weeks of dedicated support with a dedicated internal channel for bug reports.

Business Impact

55+
Users Onboarded
80%
Faster HR Processing
100%
Policy Compliance

Additional benefits: 50% reduction in HR inquiry tickets, 90% employee satisfaction rating in post‑launch surveys, and payroll errors dropped to zero.

Lessons Learned

  • Leverage existing licences: Using Power Platform within our Microsoft 365 tenant eliminated infrastructure costs.
  • Early user involvement: Involving employees from the start ensured the portal addressed real pain points.
  • Iterative delivery: Releasing modules incrementally allowed us to gather feedback and adjust quickly.
  • Documentation is key: Clear guides and training materials reduced support overhead and accelerated adoption.

Future Roadmap

  • AI‑driven anomaly detection for attendance patterns
  • Integration with external payroll systems via APIs
  • Employee self‑service for benefits and reimbursement claims
  • Advanced analytics dashboards for workforce planning
